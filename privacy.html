SIFT

Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 23, 2026

Sift is a music discovery app. Its job is to find you songs you've never heard — not to collect data about you. This page explains, in plain language, what Sift stores and what it doesn't.

What stays on your device

By default, everything. Your taste profile — the genres and artists you pick, your swipes, your saved Finds, and your settings — lives in your browser's local storage on your own device. You can use Sift fully without ever creating an account.

What we store if you create an account

Creating an account is optional. If you do, Sift stores the following, so your data survives a lost phone and friends features work:

  • Your email address and password — used only for signing in. Passwords are hashed; we never see them.
  • Your username and taste summary — your chosen handle, taste archetype, top genres and artists, and counts (e.g. how many songs you've found). Friends you add can see this.
  • Your Finds — the songs you loved, backed up privately so they can be restored on a new device. Only you can access them.
  • Friend connections and shared decks — who you've added, decks you've sent, and which songs a friend loved from your deck.
Account data is stored with Supabase, our database provider, and protected by row-level security: each user can only read and write their own data.

What we don't do

  • No ads, and no selling or sharing of your data with advertisers — ever.
  • No tracking pixels or analytics that follow you across other sites and apps.
  • No access to your contacts, location, microphone, or camera.

Music previews

Song previews, artwork, and artist data are fetched from music catalog providers (currently Deezer; Apple Music as we move to the App Store). Your requests for previews go to those services to deliver the audio; their own privacy policies apply to that delivery.

Deleting your data

  • Local data: use "Start over" on the You tab, or clear your browser data — it's gone instantly.
  • Account data: email us at vlahos89@gmail.com and we'll delete your account and everything tied to it. In-app account deletion is coming with the iOS release.

Changes

If this policy changes in a meaningful way, we'll note it here with a new date.
